After install and a first sync, you'll mostly interact with Spent in three places: the menubar app, the dashboard, and the occasional click on a transaction.

The dashboard at `http://localhost:41234` is divided into four areas:

- **Overview** - month-by-month spending totals, status pills (*on track*, *heads-up*, *over*), and the top spending categories.
- **Transactions** - a chronological list with merchant, amount, category, and date. Click any transaction to edit its category or add a note.
- **Categories** - a tree view of where your money goes. Click a category to drill down.
- **Settings** - banks, AI provider, sync schedule, and a few preferences.

## Syncing

By default, Spent syncs **once a day** at 03:00 local time, when the service is least likely to interfere with anything. You can change this in *Settings → Sync schedule*, or trigger a manual sync from the menubar app or the dashboard.

A sync does three things, in order:

1. **Scrape** - log into each connected bank and download new transactions.
2. **Deduplicate** - compare against existing transactions using a content hash (so you don't get duplicates if a bank shifts an old transaction's identifier).
3. **Categorize** - send uncategorized transactions to your chosen AI provider, in batches of 50.

If anything fails, Spent shows the error in the dashboard with a hint about what to do.

## The menubar app

The menubar app (macOS) and system tray app (Windows) give you four actions without opening the dashboard:

- **Open** - opens the dashboard in your default browser.
- **Sync now** - runs an immediate sync.
- **Start / Stop service** - pause Spent without uninstalling.
- **Quit** - quits the menubar app (the background service keeps running).

The icon turns to a small dot animation while a sync is running, and goes back to solid when done.

## Categories

Spent ships with a curated set of categories: *Groceries*, *Dining*, *Transport*, *Subscriptions*, *Shopping*, *Housing*, *Utilities*, *Health*, *Personal care*, *Entertainment*, *Travel*, *Education*, *Gifts*, *Transfers*, *Income*, *Other*. Each has a level-1 (parent) and level-2 (child) breakdown.

## Re-categorizing

Click any category badge in the transaction list to change it. Spent remembers the correction and uses it the next time it sees a similar merchant - no need to re-train.

## Dark mode

Spent ships with a built-in dark theme. Toggle it from *Settings → Preferences*, or let it follow your OS.

## Backups

Spent stores everything in `data/spent.db` (a SQLite file). To back up, copy that file plus `data/.encryption-key` to a safe place. To restore on a new machine, drop those two files into the new install's `data/` folder before the first sync.

You can also use any SQLite-friendly backup tool that handles WAL-mode databases.
